# Sleeping Heart Rate Patterns (Oura Ring Notes)

> **Source:** https://ouraring.com/blog/sleeping-heart-rate

## Overview

Your **sleeping heart rate (Resting Heart Rate - RHR)** is a valuable indicator of how well your body recovers during sleep. Rather than comparing your heart rate with others, monitor **your own baseline** and observe long-term trends.

Typical sleeping heart rate ranges from **40–100 bpm**, depending on factors such as:
- Age
- Fitness level
- Stress
- Hydration
- Overall health

---

# What to Look For

Instead of focusing only on the heart rate value, pay attention to the **shape of your overnight heart rate curve**.

### Key Indicators

- Overall heart rate trend
- Time when the lowest heart rate occurs
- Morning heart rate rise

---

# 1. Hammock Pattern ✅ (Ideal)

**Characteristics**
- Heart rate gradually decreases after falling asleep.
- Lowest heart rate occurs around the middle of the night.
- Heart rate gradually rises before waking.

**What It Means**
- Good recovery
- Healthy nervous system
- Strong sleep quality
- Healthy circadian rhythm

**Notes**
- Small heart rate spikes during REM sleep are completely normal.

---

# 2. Downward Slope Pattern 🟡

**Characteristics**
- Heart rate starts relatively high.
- Continues decreasing throughout the night.
- Lowest point occurs just before waking.

**Possible Causes**
- Eating late
- Late-night workouts
- Alcohol before bed

**Suggestion**
- Finish meals earlier.
- Exercise earlier in the day.
- Avoid alcohol close to bedtime.

---

# 3. Uplands Pattern 🟠

**Characteristics**
- Heart rate remains elevated throughout the night.

**Possible Causes**
- Illness
- High stress
- Overtraining
- Poor recovery

**Suggestion**
- Prioritize recovery.
- Reduce training intensity.
- Get additional rest.

---

# 4. Spike Pattern 🔴

**Characteristics**
- Short heart rate spikes occur during sleep.

**Normal**
- Brief spikes during REM sleep.

**May Indicate**
- Alcohol
- Caffeine
- Stress
- Illness
- Sleep disturbances

**Recommendation**
- Don't judge based on one night.
- Look for long-term trends.

---

# How to Improve Sleeping Heart Rate

- Maintain a consistent sleep schedule.
- Wake up at the same time every day.
- Avoid eating late at night.
- Limit alcohol before bedtime.
- Notice what habits produce your best ("Hammock") nights and repeat them.

---

# Key Takeaway

> The **pattern** of your sleeping heart rate is often **more informative than the actual number**.

A healthy overnight curve—especially the **Hammock pattern**—generally indicates better recovery and overall sleep quality.
